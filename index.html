Who is Don Quixote?

Alonso Quixano is a middle-aged knight who lives in an area of central Spain named La Mancha. Bewitched by the tales of chivalrous deeds in his books, he rebrands himself as Don Quixote de la Mancha takes off across Spain with his hapless squire Sancho Panza in search of adventure and glory.

History

Published in two installments in 1605 and 1615, The Ingenious Gentleman Don Quixote of La Mancha is a Spanish novel written by Miguel de Cervantes. It is one of the most translated works of all time and has been hailed as a fundamental work of Western literature.

Filled with accounts of hilarious encounters (such as Don Quixote’s fight with the giants… who were actually windmills) and the constant suffering of poor Sancho, the novel can also be highly satirical of society as Cervantes saw it.
